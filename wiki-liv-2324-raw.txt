The '''2023–24 season''' was '''[[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool Football Club]]''''s 132nd season in existence and their 62nd consecutive season in the top flight of [[Football in England|English football]]. In addition to the [[2023–24 Premier League|Premier League]], they participated in the [[2023–24 FA Cup|FA Cup]], [[2023–24 EFL Cup|EFL Cup]] and the [[2023–24 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]], having failed to qualify for the [[2023–24 UEFA Champions League|UEFA Champions League]] in the previous season.

[[Virgil van Dijk]] was appointed the new club captain after the departure of [[Jordan Henderson]]. The Dutchman also became Liverpool's second non-British or Irish captain after [[Sami Hyypiä]], who was appointed in the [[2001–02 Liverpool F.C. season|2001–02]] season. [[Trent Alexander-Arnold]] also replaced [[James Milner]] as vice-captain. The season saw a rebuild in midfield as the club signed [[Alexis Mac Allister]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12897984/alexis-mac-allister-liverpool-complete-transfer-of-world-cup-winning-midfielder-from-brighton |title=Alexis Mac Allister: Liverpool complete transfer of World Cup-winning midfielder from Brighton |publisher=[[Sky Sports]] |date=8 June 2023 |access-date=12 August 2024}}</ref> [[Dominik Szoboszlai]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11669/12912305/dominik-szoboszlai-liverpool-sign-midfielder-from-rb-leipzig-in-gbp60m-deal |title=Dominik Szoboszlai: Liverpool sign midfielder from RB Leipzig in £60m deal |publisher=[[Sky Sports]] |date=3 July 2023 |access-date=12 August 2024}}</ref> [[Wataru Endo]]<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12941826/wataru-endo-liverpool-sign-japan-midfielder-from-stuttgart-on-four-year-deal |title=Wataru Endo: Liverpool sign Japan midfielder from Stuttgart on four-year deal |publisher=[[Sky Sports]] |date=18 August 2023 |access-date=12 August 2024}}</ref> and, on transfer deadline day, [[Ryan Gravenberch]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/12951803/ryan-gravenberch-liverpool-sign-midfielder-from-bayern-munich-for-gbp |title=Ryan Gravenberch: Liverpool sign midfielder from Bayern Munich for £34.2m on Deadline Day |publisher=[[Sky Sports]] |date=2 September 2023 |access-date=12 August 2024}}</ref>

This season was the first since [[2010-11 Liverpool F.C. season|2010–11]] without [[Jordan Henderson]], the first since [[2014–15 Liverpool F.C. season|2014–15]] without [[James Milner]] and [[Roberto Firmino]], the first since [[2016–17 Liverpool F.C. season|2016–17]] without [[Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain]], and the first since [[2017–18 Liverpool F.C. season|2017–18]] without [[Naby Keïta]] and [[Fabinho (footballer, born 1993)|Fabinho]].

This was also Liverpool's final season under [[Jürgen Klopp]], who announced his departure at the season's end on 26 January 2024.<ref>{{cite news |title=Jürgen Klopp announces decision to step down as Liverpool manager at end of season |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/jurgen-klopp-announces-decision-step-down-liverpool-manager-end-season |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=26 January 2024 |access-date=26 January 2024}}</ref> On 25 February 2024, Liverpool beat [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] 1–0 in the [[2024 EFL Cup final|EFL Cup final]], with  [[Virgil van Dijk]] scoring the winner in the 118th minute to win the club's tenth League Cup and second under Klopp.<ref name="BBC Report">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/68329936|title=Chelsea 0–1 Liverpool (aet) - Virgil van Dijk extra-time winner gives Reds Wembley glory|work=BBC Sport|date=25 February 2024|access-date=25 February 2024}}</ref> It proved to be Liverpool's final trophy under Klopp; despite being in contention to win four trophies, a bad run of form in the league saw them ruled out of the title race, and they were knocked out of both the [[2023-24 FA Cup|FA Cup]] and [[2023-24 UEFA Europa League|UEFA Europa League]] in the quarter-finals by the eventual winners of their respective competitions, [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and [[Atalanta BC|Atalanta]].

===EFL Cup===
{{main|2023–24 EFL Cup}}

As one of the Premier League sides participating in European competitions, Liverpool entered the EFL Cup in the third round, and were drawn at home to Championship side [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Liverpool to face Leicester in Carabao Cup third round |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/liverpool-face-leicester-carabao-cup-third-round |access-date=30 August 2023 |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=30 August 2023}}</ref> They were then drawn away to fellow Premier League club [[AFC Bournemouth|Bournemouth]] in the fourth round,<ref>{{cite news |title=Liverpool to visit Bournemouth in Carabao Cup fourth round |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/liverpool-visit-bournemouth-carabao-cup-fourth-round |access-date=28 September 2023 |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=27 September 2023}}</ref> and at home against Premier League side [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] in the quarter-finals.<ref>{{cite news |title=Liverpool to host West Ham in Carabao Cup quarter-finals |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/liverpool-host-west-ham-carabao-cup-quarter-finals |access-date=1 November 2023 |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=1 November 2023}}</ref> In the two-legged semi-finals, they were drawn against [[Fulham F.C.|Fulham]], first leg at home then second leg away.<ref>{{cite news |title=Liverpool to face Fulham in Carabao Cup semi-finals |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/liverpool-face-fulham-carabao-cup-semi-finals |access-date=20 December 2023 |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |date=20 December 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Carabao Cup semi-final fixture details confirmed |url=https://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/carabao-cup-semi-final-fixture-details-confirmed |access-date=21 December 2023 |publisher=Liverpool FC |date=21 December 2023}}</ref>
